# DES-005 — Anthropometric Design Envelope

**Product:** HS-001 HydroSeat  
**Version:** 0.1.0  
**Status:** Draft  

## 1. Design intent

The first prototype shall prioritize a large adult user while preserving a viable adaptation path for smaller users.

The design shall not be based only on one body. The founder's dimensions are the initial upper-envelope reference, not the final population definition.

## 3. Initial dimensional envelope

Preliminary engineering range:

| Parameter | Initial range |
|---|---:|
| Seat width | 540–580 mm |
| Seat depth | 480–520 mm |
| Central opening width | 220–300 mm |
| Central opening length | 260–340 mm |
| Cushion thickness | 50–90 mm |
| Basin working depth | 40–80 mm |

These are exploration ranges, not approved specifications.

## 4. Support zones

The design shall support:

- left and right gluteal masses;
- ischial regions;
- posterior thighs without edge compression;
- lateral pelvic stability.

The design shall unload:

- perianal target region;
- central soft tissue;
- any region identified as painful during dry mock-up tests.

## 5. Adaptability strategy

Concepts to evaluate:

- removable inner inserts;
- interchangeable support rings;
- replaceable foam geometries;
- adjustable iris-like flexible interface;
- multiple basin-interface sizes;
- lateral bolsters that do not constrain large users.

## 6. Acceptance for concept phase

The envelope is adequate when:

- the founder can sit without lateral compression;
- the central opening does not carry body weight;
- a smaller-user insert concept can be demonstrated;
- thigh support remains stable;
- transfer remains safe;
- no structural element intrudes into the basin volume.
